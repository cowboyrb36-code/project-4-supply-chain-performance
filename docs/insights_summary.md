Insights Summary — Supply Chain Performance & Risk Analytics
1. Overview
This analysis evaluates end‑to‑end supply chain performance across logistics, manufacturing, supplier operations, cost structure, profitability, and risk exposure. The insights highlight operational bottlenecks, cost drivers, and improvement opportunities across carriers, suppliers, transport modes, and product categories.

The findings below summarize the most meaningful patterns discovered in the dataset.

2. Cost & Profitability Insights
2.1 Logistics and Manufacturing Costs Drive Total Cost
Logistics cost and manufacturing cost are the largest contributors to total cost across SKUs.

SKUs with high logistics cost often also show longer shipping lead times, indicating inefficiencies in routing or transport mode selection.

2.2 Profitability Varies Widely Across SKUs
Profit margins range from very low (0.4–2%) to extremely high (90%+), showing inconsistent pricing or cost control.

High‑margin SKUs tend to have:

Lower defect rates

Faster manufacturing speed

More efficient logistics routes

2.3 Profit per Unit Highlights Hidden Winners
Some SKUs with moderate revenue outperform high‑volume SKUs due to:

Lower manufacturing cost

Lower logistics cost

Higher price‑to‑cost ratio

This metric is essential for SKU‑level optimization.

3. Supplier Performance Insights
3.1 Supplier Lead Time Is a Major Bottleneck
Several suppliers show lead times above 20–30 days, directly impacting:

Production volume

Order fulfillment

Stock availability

3.2 Supplier Risk Score Correlates With Defect Rate
High supplier risk scores align with:

Higher defect rates

More “Fail” inspection results

Higher supply chain risk index

3.3 Supplier Location Patterns
Suppliers in Kolkata, Chennai, and Mumbai show the widest variation in:

Lead time

Risk score

Manufacturing delays

This suggests inconsistent regional performance.

4. Logistics & Transportation Insights
4.1 Transport Mode Strongly Impacts Cost and Lead Time
Air transport has the highest logistics cost but the lowest lead time.

Rail and Road show:

Lower cost

Higher variability in shipping time

Higher defect rates in some routes

4.2 Carrier Performance Is Uneven
Some carriers consistently show:

Higher logistics efficiency scores

Lower shipping time

Lower defect rates

Others contribute disproportionately to delays and cost overruns.

4.3 Route-Level Inefficiencies
Certain routes (especially Route A and Route C) show:

Higher shipping lead times

Higher logistics cost

Higher defect rates

These routes represent optimization opportunities.

5. Manufacturing Insights
5.1 Manufacturing Speed Bucket Predicts Cost
“Fast” manufacturing speed correlates with:

Lower manufacturing cost

Higher manufacturing performance score

Lower defect rate

5.2 High Mfg Lead Time Reduces Profitability
SKUs with long manufacturing lead times tend to have:

Higher total cost

Lower profit margin

Higher supply chain risk index

5.3 Defect Rate Is a Key Driver of Risk
SKUs with defect rates above 3–4% fall into the High Defect Risk Bucket, which correlates with:

Higher supplier risk

Higher logistics cost

Lower profit margin

6. Risk Insights
6.1 Supply Chain Risk Index Highlights High-Risk SKUs
High‑risk SKUs share common traits:

High defect rate

High supplier risk score

Long shipping lead time

High logistics cost

6.2 Supplier Risk Is the Strongest Predictor of Total Risk
Supplier risk score has the strongest relationship with:

Defect rate

Inspection failures

Manufacturing delays

6.3 Transport Mode Risk
Road and Rail shipments show higher variability in:

Shipping time

Defect rate

Cost overruns

These modes contribute more to overall risk.

7. Inventory & Demand Insights
7.1 Stock Level Variability Impacts Revenue
Low stock levels correlate with:

Lower units sold

Lower revenue

Higher stockout risk

7.2 Order Quantity Patterns
Large order quantities do not always translate to high revenue —
profitability depends more on cost structure than volume.

8. Executive Summary
The supply chain shows strong performance in some areas but faces challenges in:

Supplier reliability

Route efficiency

Transport mode variability

Manufacturing delays

High logistics cost for certain SKUs

High defect rates from specific suppliers

Addressing these issues can significantly improve:

Profit margin

Lead time reliability

Cost efficiency

Risk exposure

Customer fulfillment

This project provides a data‑driven foundation for optimizing supply chain operations end‑to‑end.
